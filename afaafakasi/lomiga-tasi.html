Gagana SÄmoa is a phonetic language, meaning words are usually pronounced as they are written. Konesona (consonants) are always followed by a vaueli (vowel) and every vaueli in a word is sounded out. LÄ'ei

LÄ'ei (clothing) is the formal word for clothes and is pronounced 'lah-??'. However, generally clothing is referred to as lÄvalava or 'ofu, pronounced 'oh-foo'. The 'ie lÄvalava, pronounced 'ee-eh la-vah-la-vah', is a wrap around rectangular printed cloth and is daily clothing item in SÄmoa.

In ancient times, clothing was made from trees such as the coconut palm and the paper mulberry. Traditional ceremonial costumes and accessories are still worn today. When linen, cotton and silk arrived in SÄmoa in the 1830s, a distinctive Samoan style of clothing emerged. The puletasi, pronounced 'poo-leh-tah-see', is a classic two piece dress worn by women and fa'afafine (third gender). Men and fa'afatama (fourth gender) wear a 'iefaitaga, pronounced 'ee-eh-fai-tah-ngah'.
